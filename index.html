About the Journey

Where glaciers meet civilization

Northern Pakistan is one of the last places on earth where ancient Silk Road culture lives on in daily practice. The Karakoram and Hindukush converge here, cradling villages that have sheltered travelers and traders for centuries.

This trip goes beyond sightseeing. We visit women's cooperatives, schools, and health centers built by the Aga Khan Development Network. We attend prayers at historic Jamat Khanas. We sit with artisans and farmers and elders.

This is a journey for people who want to understand a place, not just photograph it.

Why We Do This

We are Ismaili. We grew up hearing about places like Hunza and Gilgit as more than destinations — they are part of who we are as a community. The Karakoram is where our faith took root in mountain villages, where Jamat Khanas were built before roads existed, where women wove carpets and raised families and held things together for centuries.


This trip is not affiliated with any institution. It is just a group of us who wanted to go, and decided to bring others along. We wanted to see the AKDN projects we have heard about our whole lives. We wanted to pray in a Jamat Khana at the foot of a glacier. We wanted to sit with the women behind the cooperatives and understand what resilience actually looks like.


So we planned the trip we always wished existed. This is it.
